ABOUT PRIDE

Pride Month is a month-long celebration that recognizes the LGBTQ+ community and their contributions to society. It is a time to acknowledge the challenges faced by this community and to stand in solidarity with their fight for equality, acceptance, and human rights. Pride Month is a joyful and colorful display of love, unity, and resilience, with events and activities that promote inclusivity, educate the public, and celebrate the diversity of sexual orientations and gender identities.

BRIEF HISTORY OF PRIDE

Pride Month has its roots in the Stonewall Uprising, which occurred in New York City in June 1969. The uprising was a turning point in the LGBTQ+ rights movement, sparked by a series of protests against police harassment and discrimination. The following year, the first Pride marches were organized to commemorate the anniversary of the uprising.

In the United Kingdom, the first official Pride march took place in London in 1972. Since then, Pride Month has grown in significance, with cities across the UK hosting vibrant parades, events, and educational programs throughout June. It serves

as a platform for LGBTQ+ individuals and allies to celebrate diversity, raise awareness about LGBTQ+ rights, and foster a sense of community and acceptance.

Over the years, Pride Month has expanded to encompass a wide range of activities, including art exhibitions, panel discussions, film screenings, and concerts. These events provide spaces for LGBTQ+ individuals to express themselves freely, share their stories, and create visibility for their experiences.

WHEN AND WHERE IS PRIDE MONTH

Pride Month is traditionally celebrated in June around the world, including in the United Kingdom. In 2025, Pride Month will take place from June 1st to June 30th. This extended period allows for a wide range of events, parades, marches, and discussions to take place, providing opportunities for LGBTQ+ individuals and their allies to come together and make their voices heard.

Titled LOV3LABAN 2025, the event is happening on June 28, Saturday. The venue was not officially announced, but Pride PH tagged Quezon Memorial Circle in their Instagram post.

THE IMPORTANCE OF CELEBRATING LGBTQIA+

Celebrating Pride Month provides vital visibility for the LGBTQIA+ community and fosters acceptance. It creates a powerful sense of community and belonging, while also educating the public on the challenges LGBTQIA+ individuals still face today, including discrimination, health disparities, and violence.

Pride Month also serves as a powerful political statement, pushing for equality, legal protection, and policy change. Importantly, it honors the sacrifices and achievements of those who fought before us and those who continue to fight today.

PRIDE ORGANIZATION INTERNATIONALLY

Several organizations play critical roles in promoting LGBTQIA+ rights around the world. ILGA World is a global federation advocating for LGBTQIA+ rights in over 150 countries. Stonewall UK works toward equality and empowerment for LGBTQIA+ people across Britain and internationally.

OutRight Action International champions LGBTQIA+ human rights through direct advocacy at the United Nations and on the ground in various countries. InterPride is the international association that supports and connects Pride organizations worldwide.
